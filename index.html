  • Doba Café operates a multi-category concept spanning donuts, milk tea, coffee, breakfast, and lunch. That breadth represents a meaningful competitive advantage: most local concepts choose one lane. Doba occupies five simultaneously.
  • Customer traffic and engagement are consistent, which means demand and product-market fit are not the constraint. The business is already drawing the audience. The opportunity is in how that audience is converted, retained, and monetized.
  • Positive customer sentiment is present and documentable. High ratings and repeat behavior indicate that product delivery is sound. Guests are choosing to come back on their own without a structured system designed to bring them back.
  • The downtown location creates natural visibility and foot traffic opportunity. Social media presence is active. The external signals are favorable. The gap is in the operational infrastructure behind them.

Key Insight

Doba Café has something most early-stage concepts spend years trying to build: a product that people genuinely like and choose to return to without being asked. The challenge is not acquisition or quality. It is that every repeat visit is being generated by memory and habit rather than by a system designed to make returning easy, rewarding, and expected. Building that system against an already-warm customer base is a fundamentally different problem than building it from scratch. The foundation is there. The infrastructure is not.

02

Revenue Leak Indicators

Customer Capture Gap
  • There is no mechanism at the point of transaction that captures customer contact information. Every guest completes their visit and exits the business without entering any retention pathway.
  • Repeat visits are generated by individual memory and habit. When a regular customer stops coming in, there is no system to notice, no trigger to re-engage, and no data to indicate when the drop-off began.
  • The business has no visibility into who its customers are beyond their physical presence in the café. Each transaction is anonymous from an operational standpoint.
Impact Every customer who walks in is a one-time event until proven otherwise. A structured capture system converts that default from transactional to relational.
Menu & Offer Structuring
  • The menu covers a wide range of categories but is not architectured to guide customers toward higher-value combinations. Upsell pathways exist in the product mix but are not systematically surfaced at the point of order.
  • No defined signature offer or featured combination draws attention to the highest-margin or highest-satisfaction items. Customers are left to self-navigate a broad menu without guidance toward what the business wants them to order most.
  • Pricing and bundling are not structured to increase average ticket. A customer buying a single drink is not presented with a structured reason to add a food item.
Impact The average ticket is being set by customer defaults rather than by intentional offer architecture. A $3 increase per transaction compounds significantly at scale.
Repeat Visit Infrastructure
  • No loyalty mechanism exists to reward or incentivize return visits. A customer who comes in five times receives the same experience as a customer on their first visit. Loyalty goes unrecognized and unrewarded.
  • There is no automated follow-up path after a transaction. SMS, email, or app-based re-engagement is absent. The relationship between Doba and its guests ends at the door.
  • Seasonal or time-sensitive offers have no delivery channel. A limited donut flavor or a new milk tea launch has no way to reach customers who are not physically in the building on that day.
Impact Customer lifetime value is being left entirely to chance. The frequency and spend of repeat visits are unmanaged and untracked.
Direct Revenue Control
  • Third-party delivery platforms extract a meaningful percentage of each transaction while providing no customer data in return. The business processes the order but does not own the relationship.
  • There is no direct ordering pathway that keeps the full transaction margin in-house and captures customer information simultaneously.
  • Performance visibility is limited. Without a dashboard or structured reporting, the operator has limited ability to identify which dayparts, products, or days are driving revenue and which are underperforming.
Impact Revenue is being processed without being understood. Platform dependency reduces margin and eliminates the customer relationship that drives retention.

Opportunity Map

01The Guest Exits Without Entering the System
The Gap

Every customer transaction ends at the register. There is no in-store mechanism, QR prompt, or point-of-sale integration that captures a name, phone number, or email before the guest leaves. The business generates consistent traffic but accumulates no customer data from it. Each visit is operationally anonymous.

Why It Matters

Customer contact information is the raw material of every retention system. Without it, there is no loyalty loop to activate, no re-engagement message to send, and no way to identify which customers have stopped coming in. Every uncaptured customer is a guest the business can see but cannot reach.

Projected Impact

A structured in-store capture system converts anonymous transactions into a growing, contactable customer base with no change to existing traffic levels.

02Repeat Visits Are Earned but Not Engineered
The Gap

Customers return because the product is good and the experience is pleasant. That is a meaningful competitive position. It is not, however, a retention system. There is no loyalty mechanism, no triggered follow-up after a first or second visit, and no structured cadence that makes returning feel rewarding or expected. The business relies entirely on customer initiative.

Why It Matters

Retention is a frequency problem. A customer who visits twice per month and is nudged to three visits generates 50% more revenue from the same relationship. The difference between a casual repeat visitor and a habitual one is often a single well-timed message or a small reward. That nudge requires a system. The system does not yet exist.

Projected Impact

A structured loyalty loop and automated re-engagement sequence increases visit frequency among already-warm customers, the highest-ROI segment to activate.

03The Menu Has Five Revenue Streams. The Order Experience Uses One.
The Gap

Doba offers donuts, milk tea, coffee, breakfast, and lunch. That is a genuinely wide range. But the ordering experience does not guide customers through that range or surface natural combinations. A customer ordering a latte is not prompted to add a donut. A milk tea order does not encounter a lunch pairing. The menu exists as a list rather than as an upsell architecture.

Why It Matters

Average ticket is one of the two most controllable revenue levers in a café environment. The other is visit frequency. Both can be moved with systems rather than with increased traffic. A $3 average ticket increase across 150 weekly customers adds $450 per week before any new guests walk in. The menu already supports it. The architecture to activate it does not yet exist.

Projected Impact

POS upsell scripting and a defined signature offer structure convert existing order flow into higher average ticket without requiring changes to the menu or product mix.

04Platform Dependency is Replacing Customer Ownership
The Gap

Third-party delivery and ordering platforms process revenue but return no customer data to the business. Doba completes the order but does not know who placed it, whether they have ordered before, or how to reach them again. Each platform transaction pays a fee for the privilege of being invisible to the customer placing the order.

Why It Matters

Platform dependency compounds over time. Each transaction that routes through a third party is margin lost and a customer relationship that will never be owned. The customers using third-party platforms for Doba are demonstrably motivated. They are ordering remotely. A direct path meets them where they already are.

Projected Impact

Even a partial shift from platform to direct ordering recovers margin per transaction while simultaneously building the contactable customer base required for retention systems.

04

Operational Visibility Gaps

Performance Dashboard

No structured reporting currently exists to surface which dayparts, products, or days are generating the most revenue. Without this visibility, decisions about staffing, inventory, and promotions are made without data support. The information to build this view is generated with every transaction. It is not being captured or surfaced.

Customer Lifecycle Visibility

There is no view of where customers are in their relationship with the brand. First-time visitors, regulars, and lapsing customers all receive identical treatment. The ability to identify a guest who has not returned in three weeks, and send a message designed to bring them back, does not currently exist.

Offer & Promotion Architecture

Seasonal items, new launches, and limited-availability products have no delivery mechanism beyond physical in-store signage or organic social posts. There is no channel to announce a new product to customers who have already demonstrated interest in the brand. Promotional reach is bounded by who happens to walk in.

In-Store Conversion Flow

The physical ordering experience does not currently use team scripting or visual merchandising to guide customers toward higher-value orders. Staff interactions vary by individual rather than following a consistent, trained pattern. The point of decision, the moment a customer is deciding what to add to their order, is not currently structured to move the average ticket upward.

Closing Insight

Strategic Assessment

Doba Café already has the hardest thing to build: a product people like and a customer base that comes back on its own. That is not a given for most early-stage food concepts, and it is not something a system can create on its own. What a system can do is take that existing goodwill and make it compounding. Right now, every satisfied customer exits the transaction without entering a structure designed to bring them back sooner, spend more on their next visit, or tell someone else. The gap is not in the product or the location. It is in the infrastructure behind the front counter. A customer who visits twice a month and is nudged to three visits represents a 50% revenue increase from a relationship already earned. A $3 average ticket increase across consistent weekly traffic represents meaningful monthly lift before a single new customer walks in. These are not growth-stage problems. They are operational problems with near-term, implementable solutions. Phase One is not about changing what Doba is. It is about building the systems that allow what Doba already is to generate more predictable, more compounding revenue.
